FAQs

Open Banking is a government-backed technology that enables you to safely and securely share your financial information with authorised providers. Open Banking was introduced to drive innovation in financial services in the UK to help you more easily manage your finances, get customised deals and products, and save money. Open Banking enables you to use your own financial information to help your application – it’s just like sending bank statements, but faster, easier and more secure.​

Open Banking technology allows you to share your financial information with your chosen provider, through authorised companies. Account Information Service Providers (AISPs) are authorised by the Financial Conduct Authority. E-DocOnline as a regulated AISP, provides an online process in which you can consent to share specific financial information from your bank and credit card providers with your chosen financial services company (this could be your lender, mortgage provider, estate agent, or broker, for example). During the process, your bank will ask you to login to your online banking to verify your identity, then ask you to confirm that you’d like them to share information with E-DocOnline. Your bank only shares the specific financial information you request with E-DocOnline, and never your login details. You can connect multiple financial institutions at a time, enabling you to quickly and easily share your financial information - just as you would upload bank statements. Once you have connected your accounts, E-DocOnline consolidates all the information retrieved through Open Banking technology into a usable format for lenders, brokers, and banks. Only authorised companies can access Open Banking.

When you instruct your bank to share information with E-DocOnline, it’s like sharing the information you find on your bank statements, but easier, faster and more secure.​

If you’re asked for 90 days of consent, that means your financial services provider has asked E-DocOnline to facilitate your bank securely sharing your financial information via Open Banking with them today, and for up to the next 90 days. You’re in control - if you’d like to stop your bank sharing your account history during that 90 day period, you can revoke consent at any time by clicking the link in your confirmation email, managing your consent via online banking, or by getting in touch with E-DocOnline.​

Only E-DocOnline and the lender or company you have granted permission at E-DocOnline will have access to your financial data. We will always tell you the name of the company you will be sharing data with and exactly what data you are sharing. We only share your Open Banking data with your explicit consent. ​​

You can revoke access at any point. Your bank keeps a record of what data you’ve shared and where, and you can revoke access within your online banking at any time.

When you ask your bank to share your information with E-DocOnline, we retrieve and store the following data from your bank: Your account details (any other name by which you refer to this account, your account name, number and sort-code, your account balance, your account balance), your account transactions (details of your incoming transactions, details of your incoming transactions), and your contact details (your address, telephone numbers and email address as held by your bank/card issuer)

You have the option of choosing to manage your consent and prevent any further information being shared - you’re in control. In order to manage your consent, you can access your dashboard to view, revoke, cancel or grant new consent. ​

E-DocOnline does not store your data for any longer than is necessary, or beyond the purpose for which the data is processed. As a regulated financial institution data will be stored for a period of 7 years. This is in line with industry guidelines and also to meet any legal and regulatory obligations, such as responding to requests from the Financial Ombudsman Service, any queries you may have and any legal claims.​ ​
